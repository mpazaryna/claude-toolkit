# Expected Output Example

This is a sample of what the yoga-class-planner skill generates when given a typical prompt.

## Sample Prompt

```
@yoga-class-planner

Create a 60-minute beginner-friendly yoga class focused on hip flexibility and lower back relief.
Students have blocks and straps available. Gentle to moderate energy level.
```

---

## Sample Generated Output

# 60-Minute Beginner Yoga Class Plan
**Focus**: Hip Flexibility & Lower Back Relief
**Energy Level**: Gentle to Moderate
**Props Needed**: Yoga blocks (2 per student), straps, blankets or bolsters (optional)

---

## Opening & Centering (5 minutes)

**Easy Seated Pose (Sukhasana)** - 3 minutes

Setup:
- Sit cross-legged on mat (or on folded blanket for elevation)
- Hands rest gently on knees or in lap
- Sit tall, lengthening through spine
- Close eyes or soften gaze downward

Alignment Cues:
- Ground through sit bones
- Inhale to lengthen spine, exhale to relax shoulders
- Let breath become smooth and even
- 5-8 breaths per minute

Teaching Notes:
- Invite students to arrive on their mats
- Let go of the day
- "Notice your breath without changing it"
- Transition: "When you're ready, gently open your eyes"

**Breath Awareness (Pranayama)** - 2 minutes

- Guide students through simple breath observation
- Inhale for 4 counts, exhale for 4 counts
- 8-10 rounds
- "Set an intention for your practice - perhaps to be kind to your body"

---

## Warm-Up Sequence (12 minutes)

**Tabletop Position** - 1 minute

Setup:
- Come to hands and knees
- Wrists under shoulders, knees under hips
- Neutral spine, gaze down

**Cat-Cow Stretch (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana)** - 3 minutes

Setup:
- From tabletop, begin flowing between cat and cow
- Inhale: Drop belly, lift chest and tailbone (Cow)
- Exhale: Round spine, tuck chin and tailbone (Cat)

Alignment Cues:
- Move slowly with the breath
- Initiate movement from pelvis
- 8-10 rounds

Beginner Modifications:
- Smaller range of motion if back is sensitive
- Place blanket under knees for cushioning

Teaching Notes:
- "This mobilizes the entire spine"
- "Wakes up the muscles around your lower back"
- Common mistake: Moving too quickly

**Thread the Needle** - 2 minutes each side

Setup:
- From tabletop, slide right arm under left arm
- Right shoulder and ear rest on mat
- Left hand can stay on mat or reach toward ceiling

Alignment Cues:
- Hips stay stacked over knees
- Breathe into the stretch across shoulder blade
- Hold 8-10 breaths

Beginner Modifications:
- Place block under head for support
- Keep bottom arm extended if shoulder is tight

Teaching Notes:
- "Gentle twist for upper back and shoulders"
- "Breathe into areas of tension"
- Transition: "Press back to tabletop, switch sides"

**Child's Pose (Balasana)** - 2 minutes

Setup:
- From tabletop, bring big toes together, knees wide
- Sit hips back toward heels
- Arms extend forward or rest by sides

Alignment Cues:
- Forehead rests on mat (or on block/fist)
- Breathe into back body
- Let hips settle with each exhale

Beginner Modifications:
- Place blanket between hips and heels if knees are sensitive
- Knees closer together if hip stretch is too intense
- Block under forehead for support

Teaching Notes:
- "This is your home base - return here anytime"
- "Rest and restore"

**Downward Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)** - 2 minutes

Setup:
- From child's pose, tuck toes and lift hips up and back
- Hands shoulder-width, feet hip-width
- Create an inverted V-shape with body

Alignment Cues:
- Press firmly through hands, especially index fingers and thumbs
- Bend knees generously - focus on length in spine
- Heels reach toward floor but don't need to touch
- Hold 5-8 breaths

Beginner Modifications:
- Keep knees bent significantly
- Walk feet wider if hamstrings are tight
- Come to forearms (dolphin pose) if wrists are sensitive

Teaching Notes:
- "This is not about straight legs"
- "Prioritize a long spine over straight knees"
- Transition: "Walk feet forward slowly to meet your hands"

---

## Standing Poses (16 minutes)

**Mountain Pose (Tadasana)** - 2 minutes

Setup:
- Stand at front of mat, feet hip-width apart
- Arms by sides, palms facing forward
- Weight evenly distributed through both feet

Alignment Cues:
- Root down through all four corners of feet
- Engage thighs gently, lift kneecaps
- Lengthen tailbone down
- Roll shoulders back and down
- Reach crown of head toward ceiling
- 8-10 breaths

Teaching Notes:
- "This is the foundation of all standing poses"
- "Find your stability and center"

**Standing Forward Fold (Uttanasana)** - 2 minutes

Setup:
- From mountain pose, hinge at hips and fold forward
- Hands rest on shins, blocks, or floor
- Head and neck relax

Alignment Cues:
- Bend knees as much as needed
- Let upper body be heavy
- Breathe into back of legs and lower back
- Hold 8-10 breaths

Beginner Modifications:
- Hands on blocks (at tallest height)
- Significant knee bend
- Hands rest on shins instead of reaching down

Teaching Notes:
- "Fold from the hips, not the waist"
- "Knees can be as bent as you need"
- Releases tension in lower back and hamstrings
- Transition: "Bend knees deeply, roll up slowly to standing"

**Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I)** - 3 minutes each side

Setup:
- Step left foot back about 3-4 feet
- Turn left toes out about 45 degrees
- Bend right knee toward 90 degrees (knee over ankle)
- Square hips forward
- Arms reach overhead or rest on hips

Alignment Cues:
- Front knee tracks over second toe
- Back leg is strong and engaged
- Lengthen through both sides of waist
- Shoulders relaxed, ribs not jutting forward
- Hold 5-8 breaths

Beginner Modifications:
- Shorter stance
- Front knee bends less (70-80 degrees)
- Hands on hips instead of overhead
- Back heel slightly lifted

Teaching Notes:
- "Strong legs, open chest"
- "This builds strength in legs and opens hip flexors"
- Common mistake: Front knee collapsing inward
- Transition: "Straighten front leg, step feet together, switch sides"

**Chair Pose (Utkatasana)** - 2 minutes

Setup:
- Feet hip-width or together
- Bend knees, sit hips back as if sitting in a chair
- Arms reach forward or overhead
- Weight in heels

Alignment Cues:
- Knees track over second and third toes
- Engage core to protect lower back
- Lengthen tailbone down
- Hold 5-8 breaths

Beginner Modifications:
- Smaller bend in knees
- Hands on thighs or in prayer at heart
- Feet wider for more stability

Teaching Notes:
- "Build strength in legs and core"
- "Breathe steadily - this one is challenging!"
- Common mistake: Knees extending past toes
- Transition: "Inhale to stand, exhale fold forward"

**Tree Pose (Vrksasana)** - 2 minutes each side

Setup:
- Stand on left leg
- Place right foot on inner left ankle, calf, or thigh (avoid knee)
- Hands at heart center or overhead
- Find a steady gaze point (drishti)

Alignment Cues:
- Standing leg is strong but not locked
- Hips stay level
- Engage core for stability
- Hold 5-8 breaths

Beginner Modifications:
- Toes of lifted foot stay on floor, heel on ankle
- Hands at heart or on wall for balance
- Practice near a wall

Teaching Notes:
- "Balance is about constant small adjustments"
- "It's normal to wobble - that's your body working!"
- "Everyone's balance is different day to day"
- Transition: "Release foot down gently, pause in mountain, switch sides"

---

## Peak Pose Section - Hip Opening (12 minutes)

**Low Lunge (Anjaneyasana)** - 3 minutes each side

Setup:
- From downward dog, step right foot between hands
- Lower left knee to mat (pad with blanket)
- Hands on blocks on either side of front foot, or on front thigh
- Back toes untucked

Alignment Cues:
- Front knee over ankle (or slightly behind)
- Hips sink forward and down
- Lengthen through spine
- Option to add gentle backbend (lifting chest)
- Hold 8-10 breaths

Beginner Modifications:
- Blocks under hands for support
- Extra padding under back knee
- Less forward hip movement
- Keep hands on front thigh

Teaching Notes:
- "This deeply opens hip flexors"
- "Great for counteracting sitting"
- "Breathe into the stretch"
- Transition: "Hands frame front foot, step back to downward dog"

**Lizard Pose (Utthan Pristhasana)** - 3 minutes each side

Setup:
- From low lunge (right foot forward)
- Walk right foot to outer edge of mat
- Lower onto forearms inside front foot (or hands on blocks)
- Option: Keep back knee down or lift

Alignment Cues:
- Front foot stays flat
- Front knee tracks toward outer edge of mat
- Hip opens externally
- Breathe into outer hip
- Hold 8-10 breaths

Beginner Modifications:
- Blocks under hands/forearms (tall height)
- Back knee stays down
- Front foot stays more forward (less deep)

Teaching Notes:
- "Deeper hip opening than low lunge"
- "You might feel this in outer hip and groin"
- "Back off if it's too intense"
- Transition: "Press back up, return to low lunge, downward dog"

---

## Cool-Down & Floor Poses (13 minutes)

**Seated Forward Fold (Paschimottanasana)** - 3 minutes

Setup:
- Sit with legs extended forward
- Flex feet, toes point up
- Hinge at hips, fold forward over legs
- Hands reach toward feet, shins, or rest on blocks beside legs

Alignment Cues:
- Knees can be bent as much as needed
- Lead with chest, not forehead
- Lengthen spine with each inhale, deepen fold with exhale
- Hold 10-12 breaths

Beginner Modifications:
- Strap around feet, hold with both hands
- Generous knee bend
- Sit on folded blanket to tilt pelvis forward
- Blocks under hands beside hips

Teaching Notes:
- "This releases hamstrings and lower back"
- "Folding forward is calming for nervous system"
- "Honor your body's current flexibility"

**Seated Spinal Twist (Ardha Matsyendrasana)** - 2 minutes each side

Setup:
- Sit with legs extended
- Bend right knee, place right foot outside left thigh
- Left leg can stay extended or bend with foot near right hip
- Twist to the right, left elbow outside right knee
- Right hand on mat behind

Alignment Cues:
- Sit tall with each inhale
- Twist deeper with each exhale
- Look over right shoulder
- Both sit bones stay grounded
- Hold 8-10 breaths

Beginner Modifications:
- Bottom leg stays straight
- Less twist, use hand on floor for support
- Arm wraps around knee instead of elbow pressing

Teaching Notes:
- "Twists help with spinal mobility"
- "Wring out tension"
- "Twist from mid-back, not just neck"
- Transition: "Unwind gently, switch sides"

**Supine Pigeon (Thread the Needle)** - 2 minutes each side

Setup:
- Lie on back, knees bent, feet on floor
- Cross right ankle over left thigh (figure-4 shape)
- Lift left foot off floor
- Thread right hand between legs, left hand outside left thigh
- Hold behind left thigh or shin

Alignment Cues:
- Keep head and shoulders relaxed on mat
- Flex right foot to protect knee
- Gently draw left thigh toward chest
- Right knee opens away
- Hold 10-12 breaths

Beginner Modifications:
- Keep bottom foot on floor
- Use strap around thigh if can't reach
- Less pull toward chest

Teaching Notes:
- "Deep hip opening, especially outer hip"
- "Safe alternative to full pigeon"
- "Breathe and allow hip to release"
- Transition: "Release gently, switch sides"

**Supine Spinal Twist** - 2 minutes each side

Setup:
- Lie on back, hug knees to chest
- Let both knees fall to right side
- Left arm extends out to side
- Gaze can turn to left or stay center

Alignment Cues:
- Shoulders stay grounded
- Knees can stack or separate
- Breathe into belly and lower back
- Hold 8-10 breaths

Beginner Modifications:
- Place blanket or bolster between knees
- Block under knees if they don't reach floor
- Keep knees higher (less twist)

Teaching Notes:
- "Releases lower back tension"
- "Calming, restorative twist"
- Transition: "Draw knees back to center, switch sides"

---

## Savasana & Closing (7 minutes)

**Final Relaxation (Savasana)** - 5 minutes

Setup:
- Extend legs, feet fall naturally to sides
- Arms by sides, palms face up
- Close eyes
- Let entire body become heavy

Alignment Cues:
- Release all effort
- Let breath return to natural rhythm
- Body completely supported by floor
- Stay present and restful

Beginner Modifications:
- Blanket under head
- Bolster or rolled blanket under knees
- Eye pillow over eyes
- Blanket over body for warmth

Teaching Notes:
- "This is where your practice integrates"
- "Your body is healing and restoring"
- Guide body scan: "Release tension from feet... ankles... calves..." (continue up body)
- Near end: "Begin to deepen your breath"

**Return to Seated** - 2 minutes

Transition:
- "Wiggle fingers and toes"
- "Stretch arms overhead, full body stretch"
- "Bend knees, roll to right side"
- "Pause here for a few breaths"
- "Use your hands to press up to seated position"

**Closing** - Brief

Setup:
- Return to easy seated pose
- Hands in prayer at heart center
- Take a moment of gratitude

Closing words:
- "Thank you for your practice"
- "Namaste" (or "The light in me honors the light in you")

## Class Summary

**Total Duration**: 60 minutes
- Opening: 5 min
- Warm-up: 12 min
- Standing: 16 min
- Peak/Hip Opening: 12 min
- Cool-down: 13 min
- Savasana: 7 min

**Intensity**: Gentle to Moderate

**Props Used**:
- 2 blocks per student
- Straps
- Blankets (optional but helpful)

**Focus Areas**:
- Hip flexors (low lunge, warrior I)
- Outer hips (lizard, supine pigeon)
- Lower back (cat-cow, forward folds, twists)
- General flexibility (throughout)

**Key Benefits**:
- Releases tension from prolonged sitting
- Increases hip mobility
- Relieves lower back discomfort
- Calms nervous system
- Accessible for true beginners

**Teaching Notes**:
- Emphasize using props - they make poses accessible
- Remind students that flexibility varies day to day
- Encourage breaks in child's pose anytime
- Watch for: knees collapsing inward, overarching lower back
- Cue breath frequently

**Variations for Next Time**:
- Could add reclined bound angle pose (supta baddha konasana) in cool-down
- Could include happy baby pose for hip opening
- Could extend savasana to 8 minutes for deeper relaxation
- Could add more standing poses if students want more strength work
